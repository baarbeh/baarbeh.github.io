Jeremiah Baarbe

B.A. (Hons), M.A., JD Candidate, 2018  |  Searching for Articles

I am a University of Ottawa Common Law student interested in technology law. I have six years experience building tech to answer questions involving the law and innovation.

I work closely with Professor Jeremy de Beer and the Open African Innovation Research Network. Recent projects include co-authoring a paper on the 130-year history of IP Treaties in Africa, proposing a model license for an open data commons at IASC 2017, and conducting field research on Kenya's emerging tecnology sector. In 2016, I helped Professor de Beer intervene before the Supreme Court on the utility standard in patent law. See my publications.

I have experience leading successful teams. In 2017, I led the University of Ottawa at the Harold G. Fox Intellectual Property Moot where we won the Fox Cup for best team and the Gordon F. Henderson Award for best factum.

My skills include legal research, project management, team facilitation, business and statistical analysis, data visualization, web development, graphic design, and tutoring.
