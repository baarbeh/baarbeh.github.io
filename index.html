Jeremiah Baarbe

B.A. (Hons), M.A., JD Candidate, 2018  |  Searching for Articles

I am a University of Ottawa Common Law student interested in intellectual property, innovation, and information technology law. I have six years experience building tech to provide insight to challenging questions involving the law and innovation.

Since 2015 I have worked closely with Professor Jeremy de Beer and the Open African Innovation Research Network. My recent projects include publishing a working paper on the 130-year history of IP Treaties in Africa, proposing a licence for an open data commons, and conducting field research on makerspaces in Nairobi, Kenya. In 2016, I assisted Professor de Beer intervene before the Supreme Court in Astrazeneca v Apotex on the utility standard in patent law.

I have experience leading successful teams. Most recently, I led the University of Ottawa at the Harold G. Fox Intellectual Property Moot in February, 2017 where we won the Fox Cup for best team and the Gordon F. Henderson Award for best factum.

My diverse skills include legal research, project management, team facilitation, statistical analysis, data visualization, web development, graphic design, marketing, and tutoring.
